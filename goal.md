# Enzyme Engineering Goals for PMD (Phosphomevalonate Decarboxylase)

## Goal 1: Mitigate Cellular Toxicity

Reduce the cellular toxicity associated with the mevalonate (MVA) pathway for
isopentenol/isoprenol production. The primary toxicity source is the accumulation
of isopentenyl diphosphate (IPP), a toxic intermediate in the conventional MVA
pathway. The IPP-bypass pathway avoids IPP formation by leveraging PMD's
promiscuous activity on mevalonate 5-phosphate (MVAP), but high intracellular
MVAP accumulation (100-200 mM in vivo) causes noncompetitive substrate
inhibition of PMD, reducing its effective catalytic rate and imposing metabolic
burden on the host cell. Engineering PMD point mutations to increase the substrate
inhibition constant (Ki) for MVAP will maintain enzyme activity under high
substrate concentrations, reduce metabolic stress, and improve cell fitness during
production.

## Goal 2: Improve Catalytic Efficiency

Improve the catalytic efficiency of PMD toward the non-native substrate MVAP.
Wild-type PMDsc has approximately 35-fold lower kcat and 8-fold higher Km for
MVAP compared to its native substrate MVAPP, making PMD activity the
rate-limiting step in the IPP-bypass pathway. Engineering point mutations that
increase kcat, decrease Km, and critically increase Ki for MVAP will relieve the
pathway bottleneck and increase isopentenol titers and productivity. The combined
optimization of catalytic parameters (kcat, Km) together with substrate inhibition
resistance (Ki) is essential, as in vitro catalytic efficiency alone does not predict
in vivo performance.
